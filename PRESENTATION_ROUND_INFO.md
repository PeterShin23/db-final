# Initial Presentation Round Information for Databricks Soluations Engineer

This guide tells you everything you need to show up ready. It does not contain the scenario you will work on; you will receive that separately when your prep window opens. Read this in full now so the only thing left to focus on later is the work itself.

*Please send me your availability for the week of June 29th (excluding July 2nd and 3rd). Include as many dates and times as possible.

------------------

What this round is
------------------

A Build, Demo, Pitch! session: a working conversation with a business stakeholder, the kind a Solutions Engineer has every day. You will be given a business scenario, a short window to build a working prototype on Databricks Free Edition and prepare a short slide deck, then you will walk us through what you built and how you thought about it.

We are not looking for a polished Solutions Architect. We are looking for foundations we can build on: structured thinking, clear communication, technical curiosity, and the ability to engage thoughtfully with a hard problem.

--------------------
The shape of the day
--------------------

Two back-to-back parts on a single day:

* A 4-hour build window. You receive the scenario prompt by email exactly 4 hours before your interview. The clock starts when that email arrives. This is protected, heads-down time to build and prepare.
* A 60-minute session immediately after. Most of it is a live, customer-style conversation where you present and demo what you built. The final portion drops the simulation so we can debrief on your approach.

Plan for a 5-hour commitment in one block. Many candidates take a personal day or adjust their schedule for the headspace to do their best work. Make sure you have a quiet space and no competing obligations.

---------------------------
Scheduling and rescheduling
---------------------------

* Confirm with your recruiter that you can protect the full block on your date.
* To reschedule, tell your recruiter at least 24 hours in advance and they will move you to the next slot.
* Once your prompt has been sent, your window is live. We may not be able to reschedule after that point. This keeps the process fair for the whole cohort.

----------------------
Prepare before the day
----------------------

Do these now, not during your build window. Tooling problems on the day cut into your time, and we cannot extend it.

* Create a Databricks Free Edition account at databricks.com/learn/free-edition ( https://www.databricks.com/learn/free-edition ), confirm you can log in, and click around so the interface is familiar. All build work happens here. Play with Genie Code, our in platform coding assistant. If you cannot get access, contact your recruiter right away.
* Have your AI tools ready. Using AI is expected and encouraged in this round. Install and sign in to any tools you like to use ahead of time. The free tier of tools like Gemini and Perplexity are sufficient for this.
* Test your video setup. We use Google Meet. Before the day, confirm that screen sharing works and that you have granted your browser or app the screen-recording and microphone permissions it needs to share. Check your camera and internet too.
* Practice talking through a project in plain language. Pick something you have built and explain it out loud to someone non-technical, narrating as if sharing your screen. Watch for jargon.
* Rest. A clear head on the day matters more than last-minute cramming.

A few things to know: there is no scenario to preview, no dry runs with the hiring team, and you cannot submit work for review beforehand. You are welcome to practice general delivery with friends or family.

-------------------------
How you will be evaluated
-------------------------

75% of your score is how you think and communicate; 25% is what you built. Put most of your energy into the thinking and the story, not into building as much as possible.

* Structured thinking. Break the problem down, pick a clear focus, explain why.
* Clear communication. Plain language, no jargon, managed time.
* Business connection. Tie what you built back to a real decision the business needs to make.
* Curiosity and coachability. Stay open when challenged; be honest about what is mocked or unfinished.

On AI: you are expected to use it, and you should own every choice. "The AI suggested it" is not a reason. "I asked AI for X, reviewed what it gave me, and kept it because Y" is the ownership we want to see. Your job is to orchestrate and direct AI.

------------------
Who is in the room
------------------

The panel is acting as one curious business stakeholder. We are not a room of skeptical executives and we are not trying to trip you up. We will ask questions and push on your thinking to understand how you got there. You are not expected to have every answer; "I don't know" and "let me think about it" are valid. How you engage matters more than answering everything. This is not a technical screening.

Join your session through the Google Meet link on your calendar invite. Your recruiter is your point of contact for anything: scheduling, access, or general questions.
